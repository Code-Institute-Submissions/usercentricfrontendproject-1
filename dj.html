So you want to learn how to DJ?

Isn't it everybody's dream at some point to either be a rockstar or a DJ (or both!)? The idea of playing the music that you love to hordes of adoring fans and everything else that goes along with it. Unfortunately, I can't promise that you will become rich and famous, or even that you can make a career out of it. But what I can offer is years of experience, as someone who has spent countless hours on both sides of the DJ decks, and a clear, defined path to go from absolute beginner (or whatever starting point you are at personally) to developing the skills needed to be confident in your own abilities to play the music you love to the best of your ability. Whether you are already a weekend warrior who hangs on every mix and blend that your favourite DJ makes, or if you don't know your faders from your filters, everyone is catered to with my comprehensive DJ course. You will learn to know your equipment inside out, to the point that you could use it with your eyes closed, and you will learn how to craft consistent mixes, getting the most out of your tunes, no matter which genre(s) you enjoy playing. The course also includes constant feedback - I will be available to listen to and critique your skills as you develop them throughout the course, to offer guidance and tips along the way as your progress from being a curious casual to a demon behind the decks! Tips for aspiring DJs:

  1. Know your music! This is by far the most important thing to know for any DJ - you could have a record bag full of bangers but it's no good if you play the songs at the wrong point! Listen to your music again and again and until you are confident enough to hear a clip and know at what point in the song you are at.
  2. Deconstruct the songs in your mind down to their base elements - different drum sounds i.e kick drum and percussion, guitar, bass, synth, vocals and so on. Your aim is to mix the elements from two or more different songs together. This will help you find which songs go together and which ones clash
  3. When finding which songs work together, group them in 3s. practice mixing in and out of these songs until you are confident you can mix them whichever way. Keep doing it in threes until you have enough for a whole set, and are prepared for whatever shifts in energy that may occur.
  4. Take your listener on a musical journey - through highs and lows, and varying levels of energy. You will soon get tired if you just play high energy bangers one after the other with no lull in between! You can only appreciate the light if you have seen the darkness, and vice versa.
  5. To expand on the previous point, if you are playing to an audience, be aware of the time and the energy of the crowd - if you are playing the opening set, don't play the biggest crowd pleasers or high tempo music - not only will you tire out the crowd early on but you could also seriously annoy the other DJs or even worse. The opening DJs are supposed to ease into the right mood, not steal the show!
  6. When playing a show, bring twice as much music as you anticipate to play. You never know how the crowd might react to certain songs, however you planned the show in your head. If they love a particular song, you can play something similar. if they dislike it, you should have something a little different to fall back on and rescue the dancefloor!
  7. If you use a digital DJ controller with a computer/ laptop, use any opportunity you can to practice using other equipment (turntables/CDJs) - it never hurts to expand your skillset and you never know what equipment you might be asked to play with if you are performing elsewhere!
  8. Have fun! Everybody has their own personal tastes when it comes to music, and everyone has their own concepts of what DJing should or shouldn't be like. But you are on your own path. Don't let other people's opinions or snobbiness derail your own personal journey - you can never please everyone, so just be yourself, and most importantly - love music and enjoy yourself!.
