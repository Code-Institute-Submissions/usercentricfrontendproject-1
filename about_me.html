My name is Gareth, and I am a bank of sonic (tutorials and tips!)

Hello, and thank you for visiting my site! I have been a lifelong music lover (as have most people) - from dancing around the room to my mother's favourite records when I could barely stand, to the present day. But it was around the age 16 that I decided that I wanted to not only write my music, but also sculpt my own sounds too - inspired by the likes of Brian Eno, Jean-Michel Jarre snd John Cage. As one of my A-level options at age 16, I chose to study a BTEC in Music Technology. At this very early stage, I had no idea about any concept of electronic music production (I didn't even know what a synthesizer was - to me it was just 'keyboards!').

My first ever lesson, I was taught about drum machines. And the rest, as they say, is history. From those humble beginnings, I continued to develop my knowledge, using different DAWs (Digital Audio Workstations) until I found the right fit for me - Propellerhead's Reason. During break and lunchtimes, our teacher would let us go into the music technology room and experiment with whatever gear was lying round - anything from samplers to theremins. This grew into using the software at home and making music and learning about synthesis and other principles whenever I had the chance.

At this time I was also developing a passion for dance music, namely Dubstep and Drum and Bass music - the former, a more sparse, darker sound, the latter typically very energetic and filled with rolling percussion. As well as exploring and utilising the production techniques involved, I was listening to DJs mixing my favourite songs together seamlessly - almost creating new songs in that zone between both tracks as they took the listener (me!) on a journey. Several years later, I bought a preowned Vestax vci-100 digital dj controller, one of the first and most basic of it's kind- with no prior experience of how to mix or any of the principles of beatmatching, phrase matching, track selection and so on, my friend and I would play our favourite music together almost daily (my poor neighbours!), as we gradually were able to play well enough that we could honestly hype up each other's mixes and abilities without having to do some serious mental gymnastics!

From then until now, I have promoted my own club nights, played at club nights and house parties, produced many of my own songs and spent lord knows how many hours trying to transport the sound from my own head into the software and eventually out of my speakers. I love all kinds of music and can usually be found drumming some kind of rhythm in anticipation of my next mix or time alone with my beloved Reason. With over 10 years of experience at this point (the time has flown), I want to be able to impart what I know to anyone who is thinking of taking up either of these amazing hobbies, or even both. I have put together comprehensive courses for both disciplines to help anyone from an absolute beginner (we all have to start somewhere!) to those who have had some experience but are looking to supplement what they know and expand their own skillset.

I hope you enjoy my site; I am sure there will be something of value to you if you have landed here! I would love for you to get in touch, either via the Contact page or via the social media links in the footer of this site. Sign up to my free newsletter and you can get regular tips and tricks and free mixes, songs or sounds from myself that I wish to share with you! I wish you all the best on your musical journey and I will do whatever I can to impart whatever I know and whatever it is that will help you in your own endeavours.
